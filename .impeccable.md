## Design Context

### Users
Ambitious self-directed learners — people who buy non-fiction books to improve their work or thinking, and want to get more out of them than they do from passive reading. Used during focused daytime sessions (morning routine, lunch break, dedicated learning time). They value their time, read intentionally, and have high standards for the tools they use.

### Brand Personality
Precise, unhurried, intelligent. Not a SaaS dashboard — more like a trusted editorial companion. The interface should feel like it was designed by someone who reads seriously, not by a startup. Confident without being loud.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial / magazine-like. Think *The Atlantic*, *Five Books*, or a premium reading app like Readwise Reader. Rich typography, generous white space, warm off-white surfaces. Left-aligned layouts with strong typographic hierarchy. The typography does the work — not decoration, not gradients, not cards-on-cards.

- **Theme**: Light (daytime use, premium reading context)
- **Background**: Warm cream (not clinical white)
- **Accent**: Deep teal-green — intellectual, distinctive, nothing like generic SaaS indigo/blue
- **Fonts**: Literata (editorial serif for headings, book titles, module titles) + Figtree (geometric humanist sans for UI chrome, body text)
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS (Notion clones, dashboard apps), anything with glowing accents, gradient text, or colored card stripes

### Design Principles
1. **Typography carries the weight** — font choice, size contrast, and spacing do more work than color or decoration
2. **Warm over clinical** — cream backgrounds, tinted neutrals, nothing pure black or pure white
3. **Restraint as a statement** — fewer visual elements, more breathing room; what's removed matters as much as what's added
4. **Editorial hierarchy** — module numbers are large and expressive, book titles are prominent, labels are quiet
5. **Status without noise** — progress and completion states shown through subtle indicators, not colored chips or badges
